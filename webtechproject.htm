MALABAR CHICKEN DUM BIRIYANI




Malabar chicken dum biryani is a popular Indian dish, particularly in the South Indian state of Kerala. 
It is a flavorful and aromatic dish that consists of long-grain basmati rice cooked with chicken, 
spices, and herbs. The dish is typically made by layering the chicken masala and partially 
cooked rice in a pot or pan, and then sealing it with a lid and cooking 
it over a low flame. This slow-cooking process allows the flavors to blend together 
and results in a rich and delicious biryani.The Malabar region of Kerala is known for 
its unique blend of spices, which are used in this dish to give it a distinct and flavorful taste. 
The chicken is typically marinated in a spice mix and then cooked with onions, 
tomatoes, and other spices until it is tender and juicy. 
The rice is cooked separately with a blend of spices and herbs, and then 
layered with the chicken masala to create a delicious biryani that is perfect 
for special occasions or as a hearty meal.


Ingredients:

	• 2 cups basmati rice
	• 500g chicken, cut into small pieces
	• 2 onions, sliced thinly
	•2 tomatoes, chopped
	•1/2 cup yogurt
	•1/4 cup ghee
	•1/4 cup vegetable oil
	•1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
	•2 bay leaves
	•4-5 cloves
	•4-5 green cardamom pods
	•2-3 cinnamon sticks
	•1 teaspoon red chili powder
	•1 teaspoon coriander powder
	•1 teaspoon cumin powder
	•1 teaspoon garam masala powder
	•Salt to taste
	•Handful of mint leaves
	•Handful of coriander leaves

	

Instructions:

	1.Wash the basmati rice and soak it in water for 30 minutes.
	2.In a large pot, heat the ghee and oil on medium heat.
	3.Add the cumin seeds, bay leaves, cloves, cardamom pods, and cinnamon sticks. Fry for a few seconds 
	  until fragrant.
	4.Add the sliced onions and sauté until they are golden brown.
	5.Add the chopped tomatoes and cook for a few minutes until they are soft and mushy.
	6.Add the chicken pieces, yogurt, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, 
	garam masala powder, and salt. 
	Mix well and cook for 10-15 minutes on medium heat until the chicken is cooked through.
	7.In a separate pot, boil 6-7 cups of water with salt and add the soaked rice. 
	Cook until the rice is partially cooked, then drain the water.
	8.Layer the chicken masala and rice in a large pot or deep dish. Add a handful of mint leaves 
	and coriander leaves on top of each layer.
	9.Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or seal with foil. Place the pot on a tawa or 
	griddle and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes until the rice is fully cooked and fluffy.
	10.Garnish with additional mint leaves and coriander leaves. Serve hot with raita or pickle.

	
Note: The recipe can be adjusted according to personal preferences, such as adding 
    	more or less spices or adjusting the cooking time.
