SOYA CHUNKS




Soya chunks are a vegetarian protein-rich food that is made from defatted soy flour. 
They are also known as soy nuggets or textured vegetable protein (TVP). 
Here's a recipe to make Soya Chunks Masala:



Ingredients:


- 1 cup soya chunks
- 2 tbsp oil
- 1 onion, finely chopped
- 1 tomato, finely chopped
- 1 tsp ginger paste
- 1 tsp garlic paste
- 1 tsp cumin seeds
- 1 tsp coriander powder
- 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
- 1 tsp red chili powder
- Salt to taste
- 1 tbsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
- 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves for garnishing


	

Instructions:

1. Boil the soya chunks in hot water for 10-15 minutes until they become soft and tender.
2. Drain the water and rinse the soya chunks under cold water to remove any excess starch.
3. Squeeze out the water from the soya chunks and keep them aside.
4. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
5. Add finely chopped onions and saute until they turn translucent.
6. Add ginger paste, garlic paste, and chopped tomatoes. Mix well and cook until the tomatoes 
turn soft and mushy.
7. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
8. Add the boiled soya chunks and kasuri methi. Mix well and cook for another 5-7 minutes.
9. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or roti. Enjoy your Soya Chunks Masala!

	
Note: The recipe can be adjusted according to personal preferences, such as adding
more or less spices or adjusting the cooking time.
