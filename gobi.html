GOBI MANCHURIAN







    
Gobi Manchurian is a popular Indo-Chinese dish that consists of crispy fried 
cauliflower florets tossed in a spicy, tangy sauce. 
Here's a recipe to make it at home:



Ingredients:


-1 head cauliflower, cut into small florets
-1/2 cup all-purpose flour
-1/4 cup cornstarch
-1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
-1 tsp soy sauce
-1/2 tsp red chili powder
-Salt to taste
-Oil for frying
-1 tbsp oil
-1 onion, chopped
-1 capsicum, chopped
-2 green chilies, chopped
-1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
-2 tbsp soy sauce
-1 tbsp tomato ketchup
-1 tbsp vinegar
-1 tsp sugar
-1/2 tsp red chili powder
-1 tbsp cornstarch dissolved in 1/4 cup water
-Salt to taste
-Chopped spring onions for garnish



    
Instructions:

In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, cornstarch, ginger-garlic paste, soy sauce, 
red chili powder, and salt. Add enough water to make a thick batter.
Dip cauliflower florets in the batter and coat well.
Heat oil in a deep pan and fry the cauliflower florets until golden brown.
Remove and place on a paper towel to absorb any excess oil.
In a separate pan, heat oil and sauté onion, capsicum, green chilies, and ginger-garlic 
paste until the onions are translucent.
Add soy sauce, tomato ketchup, vinegar, sugar, red chili powder, and salt to the pan. Mix well.
Add the cornstarch-water mixture to the pan and stir until the sauce thickens.
Add the fried cauliflower florets to the pan and toss until the florets are coated in the sauce.
Garnish with chopped spring onions and serve hot.
Your delicious Gobi Manchurian is now ready to be enjoyed!


    Note: The recipe can be adjusted according to personal preferences, such as adding
    more or less spices or adjusting the cooking time.
